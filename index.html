About me

I am a full stack react engineer with a background in healthcare. I have always had a passion for technology since I was a child. I grew up with a mind for engineering. When I would receive a new console or gaming system, I would always try to take it apart to figure out how it worked. Overall, I would typically break the system but my curiosity never went away. As I grew, I learned more about how to put it all back together. I would perform regular maintenance on my consoles, computers and even vehicles just to learn more about how everything worked. I always had this passion in the back of my mind even as I pushed through my education in healthcare. As I began my career in healthcare, my interest in technology never went away. I began by rebuilding an old broken computer tower. This computer became my project computer. I had spent hours self-teaching myself how to use C++. As time went on, I found less time to work on coding. A few years later, I found the coding boot camp through University of North Carolina at Chapel Hill. At this point of my life, I knew this is what I wanted to do. Six months later, my suspicion was confirmed as I pushed through the boot camp and rekindled my love of building applications from the ground up.

I have spent the last 5 years in healthcare. I have enjoyed my experiences and the knowledge I have gained from this. However, I am ready to move into a new adventure. Prior to my healthcare time, I worked at the International Business Machines (IBM). I spent 3 years working in computer chip fabrication, also called the Fab. I learned many different machines. My love for technology grew during this time. While I was working at IBM, I was continuing my education for healthcare. I spent my day in class and worked 12 hour night shifts.
